NASA Deputy Inspector General

George A. Scott

George A. Scott is the Deputy Inspector General for the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA). As the Deputy Inspector General, Mr. Scott assists the Inspector General in managing the full range of programs and activities in the NASA Office of Inspector General (OIG).

Mr. Scott has over 30 years of experience at the Government Accountability Office (GAO), where he served as the Managing Director of GAO’s Homeland Security and Justice Team. In this role, Mr. Scott managed GAO’s work on Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and Department of Justice programs and operations. This included DHS efforts to secure our nation’s borders and infrastructure; and strengthen emergency preparedness and response capabilities. Mr. Scott was responsible for maintaining effective communications and collaboration with senior DHS officials. Mr. Scott also led GAO's work on Justice programs and grants to strengthen the judicial system to more effectively address crime, illegal drug use, illegal immigration, and improve judiciary and prison operations.

Prior to becoming Managing Director, Mr. Scott was responsible for directing GAO’s engagements on education issues. His work addressed topics of great interest to the American public and congressional clients. This included reviews focused on Race to the Top and other school reform initiatives, Indian education, charter schools, STEM programs, students with disabilities, and federal student aid programs.

Early in his GAO career, Mr. Scott led a body of work on employee benefit plans, including reviews of the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation, Labor and IRS oversight of pension plans, and the federal Thrift Savings Plan. Mr. Scott's work resulted in important legislative and regulatory changes as well as improved accountability for federal programs and funds.

Mr. Scott graduated from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. He has received several GAO honors including the Distinguished Service Award, Meritorious Service awards, Client Service Award, the John Henry Luke Mentoring Award, and the Richard J. Hillman Human Capital Award.
